Key details

Schedule

  • Look for emails regarding practice, meets, and other events
  • Details on timing of practice, meets, socials, events, etc. can be found bit.ly/farm_y_cal
  • How to add this calendar to Apple devices bit.ly/how_to_add
  • Link to use when adding it here
  • By linking, any updates we make will automatically show up on your calendar
  • This may or may not be helpful
  • You don't need to add the calendar to see it

Swim suits

  • They are cool; it's hard to not love them
  • Can be ordered during suit fittings
  • Not required though
  • However, don't wear other teams' gear; this might disqualify (DQ) your swimmer
  • Schedule has dates of suit fitting

Practice

  • Swimmers to have suit, goggles, cap on 5 minutes before practice start
  • Stay for entire practice session for that age group
  • Please come to practice as often as feasible for your family:
    • The more coaches know your swimmer, the more informed they are likely to be when deciding individual and relay event assignments at meets
  • Our coaches are awesome and experienced. Please give them space during practice and stay on the upper deck

Meets: before the meet

  • Individual event signup requests for swimmers can be done via the links sent via email
  • Needs to be done well before the meet
  • Double check the signups before the meet and back out of an event if you can longer attend
  • Sign up to volunteer for meets using links sent via email

Meets: day of the meet

  • Check in with a chaperone assigned to your swimmer's age group and gender; please do this first
  • Please remind your swimmers to stay in their age group assignment
  • For the younger age groups, please ask your swimmer to be escorted to a restroom if they need to use one
  • Check in with chaperones before leaving
  • Have fun and let the swimmers have fun

Meet: event assignments

  • Swimmers are assigned to events based on the priorities and discretion of the coaches
  • Parents, volunteers, parent reps can make requests for individual events (for inclusion or for exclusion)
  • Relays
    • Relays, for obvious reasons, are highly sought after
    • The number of relay slots are limited, however
    • Relay victories lead to a higher point differential

Meet: event assignments

  • For individual and relay events, coaches are likely to take the above among a range of other factors into consideration

  • Bottomline: it's the coaches call

Volunteer roles

  1. Timers: ~20 per meet
  2. Chaperones: ~12 per meet
  3. Clerk of course: ~3 per meet
  4. Scorers: ~2 per meet
  5. Runners: ~2 per meet
  6. Card keeper: ~2 per meet
  7. Ribbon distributor: 1 per home meet
  8. Ribbon writer: ~2 after every meet
  9. Stroke and turn judge: ~2 per meet
  10. Runner: 1 per meet
  11. Scorer: 1 per meet

Volunteer roles

  • Adding up the numbers, that's ~50 people per meet
  • It takes more than just a little bit to run a meet
  • Do help out if you have the time
  • There are incentives for volunteering
  • Ask one of the parent reps during practice

Security

  • We rely on parents and volunteers to be watchful and expect them to voice concerns
  • To add an extra layer of security, we are making a change this year
  • For events hosted at the Farm, someone from the Farm staff will be positioned at the swimming pool entry gate

Inclement weather announcements

  • Please monitor your email for last minute changes to practice because of inclement weather
  • We are also planning on sending text messages this year as an added convenience
  • Please email uncfarm@hotmail.com to be added to text message alerts list
